# keju-examiner

[中文](README.md) | English

I uploaded the data of Qing dynasty imperial examination examiners that I collected when I didn't want to study.
It was copied from [_Qing Mi Shu Wen_](https://www.shidianguji.com/book/CADAL11202250/chapter/1lk09dczg8sun?page_from=searching_page&version=3) and
[_Qing Mi Shu Wen Xv_](https://www.shidianguji.com/book/CADAL11202250/chapter/1lk09dczg8sun?page_from=searching_page&version=3).
Some variables were simply extracted using DeepSeek. You can find it in [data](./data). 

I have always been interested in the selection process of the imperial examination examiners.
In fact, serving as an examiner for an imperial examination (either the provincial or the national) was a job that yielded much more benefits than costs.
After an examination, the students who passed would become students of examiners, providing significant assistance to their future career.
Under such circumstances, who were selected as examiners is an interesting matter. Would various forces engage in a game for it? Would the emperor balance things?
However, I cannot find a good empirical point. So, let's just leave it for now.
